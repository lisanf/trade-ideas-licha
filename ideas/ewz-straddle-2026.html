EM Equities · Brazil · Options Updated Mar 2025

Trade the path, not the direction. If Brazil politics drives larger-than-expected moves, or implied vol ramps into the vote, volatility buyers get paid.

The Context

If you have ever traded EM around a political event, you know the rhythm: weeks of quiet, then a single poll headline and everything gaps at once. Brazil's 2026 presidential election is scheduled for October 4, with a potential runoff on October 25 if no candidate clears 50% outright. Recent polling points to a tight runoff scenario; exactly the kind of setup that tends to keep volatility bid for longer than the market expects.

This is not a call on EWZ up or down. It is a bet that the options market is underpricing how much noise a polarized political field generates in the months leading into October. The underlying can go anywhere — the trade just needs it to move.

Implementation

The cleanest institutional expression of a long-volatility view like this is a variance swap, an OTC derivative that settles on realized variance versus a fixed strike, with no upfront premium (though margin/collateral applies). If you have ISDA documentation, that is typically where this conversation starts.

For a transparent, accessible example anyone can follow, the vehicle here is a straddle, buying a call and a put at the same strike simultaneously. The straddle is not the idea. It is just a visible, liquid tool to express the same view: long volatility, no directional bias.

How the Trade Makes Money
01
The big move

EWZ breaks hard in either direction, a tail outcome. The winning leg grows faster than the losing leg decays. Classic straddle payoff.

02
The IV ramp

Often the more realistic path. Implied vol gets bid up into the election as market participants pay for hedges. You sell before expiry at a higher premium than you paid.

Brazil Elections — October Pattern

Brazil's presidential elections have consistently landed in early October since the constitution was adopted, with a runoff three to four weeks later when no candidate wins an outright majority. That rhythm is structural, not coincidental, and it is why the November expiry exists as a trade.

Year Round 1 Runoff Days Between
2014 Oct 5 Oct 26 21
2018 Oct 7 Oct 28 21
2022 Oct 2 Oct 30 28
2026 upcoming Oct 4 Oct 25 21

2022 was the anomaly, 28 days between rounds, one of the closest elections in Brazilian history, with EWZ swinging through the entire period. That is the kind of realized move this trade is anticipating a repeat of.

What You Tell a PM

This is expensive volatility. The options market is effectively charging 22–26% for the move, that is what the break-even range implies if you hold to expiry. That is a high hurdle, and it is worth saying clearly.

  • Vol crush: if the first-round result is clean and decisive, implied volatility can collapse immediately. The event resolves, the bid for optionality disappears, and you are left holding expensive paper.
  • Execution: "last" prices are not tradable in size. Bid/ask spreads on EWZ options widen on lower-liquidity strikes. The numbers here are indicative — real entry cost is higher.
  • Carry: the Nov expiry costs more, and every day without a vol move is a day of theta decay working against you.
  • This is not a hedge fund: a variance swap would be cleaner, cheaper in spread terms, and have better convexity. The straddle is the retail-accessible version of the same view.
The Game Plan
A
First-round focus

Oct expiry. Cheaper entry, cleaner event exposure. Preferred if the goal is to trade the initial shock and not overpay for extra time.

B
Full cycle

Nov expiry. Pays for the runoff and the post-election noise. Preferred if the view is that the market will take more than one round to price the outcome correctly.

C
IV ramp exit

Neither expiry requires holding to expiry. If implied vol spikes into the election and the market starts paying up for uncertainty, selling the position at a higher premium is the cleaner outcome than waiting for a binary result.
