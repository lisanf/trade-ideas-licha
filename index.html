The Thesis

Binary Event Catalyst

Brazil's election is a genuine detonator. Lula vs. Flávio Bolsonaro in a razor-thin potential runoff keeps headlines explosive from July through end-October. Sustained political tension means implied vol stays elevated far longer than a single-day event spike.

↕
Zero Directional Bias

A straddle profits massively from movement in either direction. A right-wing win could ignite EWZ on reform euphoria. A left-wing result could send it into freefall. We are not guessing the outcome. We are betting on the storm itself.

📈
Two Paths to Profit

Hold through the explosion if EWZ makes its move, or sell the straddle pre-vote when the market bids implied vol into the stratosphere. The IV ramp trade has historically delivered sharper risk-adjusted returns than waiting for realised carnage.

The Perfect Instrument

🏦 Institutional Only · OTC

For those who want pure volatility — with no noise attached — the ideal instrument is not a straddle. It is a Variance Swap.

A straddle is accessible and liquid. But it comes with baggage: delta drift, skew exposure, bid-ask friction across two legs. A variance swap strips all of that away. You trade realised variance directly against a fixed strike. Direction is completely irrelevant. If Brazil explodes in either direction — or just thrashes incessantly through the election window — the payoff compounds convexly in your favour. No hedging. No rolling. No strikes to manage. Just volatility, in its purest form.

The convexity is the killer feature: gains accelerate as vol surges, while losses are cushioned if vol disappoints. It is the most efficient way to be long the chaos of a contested South American election.
